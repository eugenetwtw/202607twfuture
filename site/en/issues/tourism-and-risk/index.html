04 Tourism, insurance, and risk

High-risk sports need insurance and a rational culture.

Switzerland is mountainous: skiing, cable cars, lodging—and wingsuit flying that kills people every year. What kind of country tolerates people from everywhere flying in the Alps and sometimes hitting a mountain?

Those activities both hit and feed tourism, accident and life insurance, and related services. Risk is not erased; it is priced, insured, and woven into industry chains.

Would Taiwanese accept, as Switzerland does, letting locals and foreigners play death games in the mountains? Usually the answer is no. That is not only regulation—it is a deeper culture of responsibility.

Among green and high-end services, tourism mattered enormously before COVID: low carbon, less clash with land and factories. Switzerland binds scenery, insurance, and service into one system.

If we talk about ‘rationality,’ we cannot only chant progress. We must ask what mechanisms society will use to bear risk—and which industries we forfeit when we refuse.
