31 Culture export

Culture is GDP—and daily beauty.

Swiss literature has limited global fame, but the Lucerne Festival is a European pillar: relatively low public subsidy, while visitor food and lodging make the tax logic work. Culture can be industry, not pure spend.

In New York in 2013, The Lion King and Phantom played every night—the city turned stories into ticketed infrastructure.

Taichung has the National Opera House and flower markets: life can be cheap and beautiful. Only on the campaign trail did I walk markets heavily and see aesthetics overlap with electoral geography.

Taiwan’s history is short; literary and translation export is still thin. That is not a curse—it is investable blank space.

Culture export sits with tourism, education, and urban regeneration. When cities only pour concrete and do not grow content, an opera house is a large air-conditioned room.
