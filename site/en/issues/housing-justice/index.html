18 Housing justice

Justice needs supply design, not slogans.

Taiwan often believes government must provide very cheap housing. If it truly floods the market at low enough prices, homes may stratify into ghettos—or shock existing property and bank loans, stop builders, leave unfinished towers, and hurt the vulnerable more.

Talking justice while refusing market consequences is vote fraud. The real question is how to expand affordable supply without detonating financial stability.

Switzerland’s lesson is a deep, relatively affordable rental market—not only government building until prices halve. Taiwan still releases too little public land and housing into rental.

In Taichung’s seventh redevelopment zone, some builder land still sits empty; supply, tax, lending, and urban-renewal rules shape young people’s futures more than any single slogan.

Housing justice is an engineering and institutional problem. It needs honest quantity models, not only team-picking on social media.
