Issue 24 ≈ 2–3 min read From the book: 6. AI, IoT, remote work & justice

24 Remote work and AI evaluation

Manage outcomes, not chair time.

Companies like Zapier run fully remote with staff worldwide. The point is not romance but management: leaders need information systems, security, and people skills.

The worst culture is ‘you weren’t at the Taipei meeting so you don’t know.’ Remote and on-site must share the same information access; hallway decisions after meetings systematically hurt the absent.

What you save is not only fuel but family time—productivity and the happiness economy can share one path.

The pandemic made work-from-home overnight normal; the shift will not fully reverse. AI and vision can analyze attendance and focus, but the core should still be outcomes and goals—not finer surveillance.

The public sector can imagine shared neighborhood offices so civil servants work nearby. Tech is ready; missing pieces are trust architecture and manager habits.
