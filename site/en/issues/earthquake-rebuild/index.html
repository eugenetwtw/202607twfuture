19 Seismic safety and rebuild

Old buildings are a public-safety issue.

On 6 February 2018 the Hualien quake collapsed multiple buildings; Yunmen Cuiti alone killed 14. I took my children east for seven days of survey—collapse is not a news frame; it is shared failure of neighbors and structure.

Many early Taiwan buildings used standard drawings to skip architects and process costs. Times changed: seismic demands and use patterns changed. What must be rebuilt cannot forever hide behind nostalgia.

In March 2020 I inspected a new house near a fault. After the pandemic we should ask harder: risk disclosure, design liability, and renewal incentives—are they aligned with public safety?

Engineers see members and load paths; society sees price and school districts. Without translation between them, tragedy does the translating.

Rebuild is not another name for speculation. It is moving people out of unacceptable risk—with policy and technique, not prayer.
