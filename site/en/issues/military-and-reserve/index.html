28 Service and reserve forces

Copy principles, not templates.

Swiss men at twenty serve about 260 days, often in stages. Reserves let standing forces expand quickly—a small state’s craft of survival.

I drew the Marine Corps; my father apologized, I did not mind. Taiwan’s service length has been revised many times; streamlining and alternative service changed the social experience of ‘all-people defense.’

Taiwan’s threat differs from Switzerland’s: sea on all sides, mainly air and naval risk rather than tanks over a land border. Unlike the two Koreas—Southern firms do not build plants in the North—Taiwanese companies are deep in the other shore’s supply chains.

Defense is strategy and industry, not only slogans and half-year training nostalgia. Whether reserves truly mobilize, logistics and civil defense exist, matters more than posters.

A small state’s rationality is to see its own battlefield shape, then set service and budgets. Copying Switzerland—or any other country—will distort in the weather of the Strait.
