20 TDR and education funds

Sell density rights, invest the principal, fund education with yield.

Heritage preservation often uses transferable development rights: sell development rights for cash. If cash is spent as subsidy, it vanishes in smoke; if used to buy assets (bonds) and fund education with yields, the logic is entirely different.

What we need most is education. Talent is future tax base; investment in young citizens should, in logic, return.

TDR policy often drowns in ‘renewal / preservation / windfall’ noise. Fewer ask about post-cash governance: who manages, what is bought, who receives the yield.

Turning one-time development gain into a lasting education cash flow is long-termism a city can choose.

Buildings age; children grow. Public finance that only spends the present leaves the next generation slogans.
