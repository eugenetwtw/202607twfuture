29 Nation and identity

Heidi’s origins mirror Taiwan’s identity tension.

Switzerland’s federal constitution recognizes multiple national languages. Are they German or Swiss? Are we Taiwanese or Chinese? The structure of confused identity is similar; the historical roots are not.

Fifty years of Japanese rule left rural and Indigenous communities that may still understand Japanese; Hokkien, Hakka, and postwar Mandarin layered on. As a child I was punished for Taiwanese; without grandparents, my children may lose that language environment.

In 2010 media stirred when scholars suggested Heidi’s story may stem from German texts—Swiss hearts ached. A fictional character’s nationality can still prick real national nerves.

Where Mazu came from, which tongue is ‘national language,’ whether passport and self-identification match: elections will not settle these.

Identity needs narrative and daily life. Multilingual, multi-origin countries are not defects—Switzerland proved they are governable if institutions admit complexity instead of forcing singularity.
