13 Time-of-use pricing

Without price signals, storage and renewables stall.

Time-of-use pricing turns when you use power into cost. For households it is a bill detail; for the grid it is dispatch language: peaks expensive, off-peak cheap—so storage and flexible load have a reason to exist.

V2G finance depends on peak/off-peak spreads. Flatten the signal and the ‘car as power plant’ story stays a slide deck.

Unstable renewables need demand and storage to dance together. Price is the cheapest conductor—more scalable than endless subsidies or administrative assignment.

Tariff reform is often politicized as ‘everyone pays more.’ The real design is who wastes power at peak, who provides flexibility, and who should be rewarded.

Without clear time-of-use prices, energy transition is like trade without an exchange rate. Let prices speak so technology and capital can follow.
