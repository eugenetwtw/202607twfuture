23 Fintech and blockchain

From stages to sandboxes: make it industry.

In 2017 I spoke at a Seoul fintech event; day one I was almost the only keynoter without a booth. They were keen on our blockchain work—behind technical interest sit industrial anxiety and opportunity.

Southeast Asia’s ICO and crypto markets often coexist with scams because legal investment channels are thin. The logic: if you can do higher-grade, more elegant work, why abandon markets where crude plays already suck capital?

Taiwan can sandbox virtual-asset management: custody, yield, collateral lending, investment guarantees. Traditional finance will not quickly beat Shanghai, Hong Kong, or Singapore; virtual assets are a window.

Blockchain’s promise to cut middlemen belongs in real flows: clearing, identity, contracts, regulatory reporting—not only coin-price charts.

An engineer nation that only builds hardware will cede software and financial protocols to others. Implementation is the only moat.
