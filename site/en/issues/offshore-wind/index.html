11 Offshore wind and liberalization

Sea is the asset; markets make it real.

Unlike landlocked Switzerland, Taiwan is surrounded by sea. Offshore wind is irrelevant for them and structural for us. Turbines stand offshore without fighting residents for land; dolphins may object—but dolphins do not vote.

Is strait wind enough? Build and we will know. Capital is mostly foreign and project finance; if the system cannot deliver, money will not keep coming. Better an open platform for talent and capital than endless abstract debate.

Feed-in tariffs are often attacked as games for the rich or the ruling party. One path is energy liberalization: factories need not buy only from Taipower; Taipower becomes more like a network and dispatcher while renewables and other supply compete.

Offshore wind is heavy engineering that needs time to prove itself—but ‘Taiwan has a sea’ needs no more proof. What is missing is reason and predictable rules.

Wind also needs storage and price signals—or unstable green power never becomes reliable daily supply. That is the same problem set as V2G and time-of-use pricing.
