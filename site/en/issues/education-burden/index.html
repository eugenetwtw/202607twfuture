Issue 30 ≈ 2–3 min read From the book: 5. Culture & education

30 Schoolbags and capture

Heavy bags are a system failure.

From preschool through compulsory schooling, Taiwan is stuffed with rote load. Under one curriculum and many textbooks, books invade backpacks; parents let agencies and publishers capture children—bags heavier than my briefcase.

Content could be ebooks, but industry fears piracy and still prices physical objects—so material you must touch counts as money. If Netflix-style knowledge subscriptions matured, weight and paper cost could fall.

In 2020 global school closures forced remote teaching. If parents can see live or recorded classes, quality under permanent tenure would be shaken—and improved.

Swiss paths are more plural: clearer academic and applied tracks. Taiwan’s tech-university upgrades are still young; industry–academia practice culture is not yet stable.

Education is a low-carbon industry and future tax base. Lightening bags and raising remote and international-student share looks more modern than printing thicker volumes.
