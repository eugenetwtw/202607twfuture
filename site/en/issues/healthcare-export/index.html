06 Exporting Taiwan healthcare

Universal coverage is an edge—export it.

Switzerland leads Taiwan in many fields, but on healthcare access and NHI economics Taiwan can be proud: relatively low premiums, relatively abundant resources.

U.S. care can be excellent yet inaccessible—a child’s routine check may need three months’ booking. Taiwan is another model: people can get in, and they can afford it.

The 2020 pandemic put masks on the world and put Taiwan’s medical and public-health capacity on display. The question is whether we can and will turn that capacity into exportable services and institutional products.

Export is not merely ‘selling doctors.’ It is the full package: insurance, telemedicine, SOP, trust, and language interfaces. Switzerland exports finance and precision; Taiwan could export accessible health.

As manufacturing chains reconfigure, care is one of the few paths with both human value and economic potential—if we treat our advantage as strategy, not as everyday entitlement we take for granted.
