15 Nuclear power and rationality

Fear must be heard—but not replace arithmetic.

Nuclear power in Taiwan is highly politicized. Fear comes from dense-island risk imagination and long political marketing. Right or wrong, the marketing worked—fear became collective intuition.

Mothballing nuclear may cost on the order of US$1,000 per person (a rough estimate in the book). That ledger rarely sits next to the sentence ‘anti-nuclear equals moral correctness.’

China’s coast near Taiwan hosts many reactors. The book’s harsh analogy: when the whole class cheats, playing pure may make you the model student—or the first expelled. Energy geopolitics is not a civics lesson.

I want cooler discussion: risk, waste, baseload, carbon, imported fuel, grid stability—item by item, not ended by labels.

Romantic Taiwan needs an engineer’s calm. Breathable air and accountable power prices are both parts of rationality.
