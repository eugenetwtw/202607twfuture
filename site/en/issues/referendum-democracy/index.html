27 Referendums: democracy or manipulation

Referendums need design, not romance.

Swiss citizens can, within a window, collect signatures to put a passed law to a veto referendum. Relative to total citizens, the threshold is not astronomical. Those who fear referendum instability are told to look at Switzerland: high power and high income can coexist.

The 2016 Brexit vote under Big Ben remains parallel stories of ‘democratic victory’ and ‘populist manipulation.’

How questions are worded, information parity, thresholds, binding force, and handover with parliament decide whether results are deliberation or emotion.

Taiwan’s referendum experience and two-party structure make reform hard—but not empty: move citizen power toward operable, verifiable design, not only mobilization season.

Democratic maturity often shows in whether we dare put the knife in the people’s hands—and whether we designed the handle well.
