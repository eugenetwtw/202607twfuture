12 V2G and anti-pollution EV bonds

EVs as grid storage—and family cars.

V2G (vehicle-to-grid) describes the bond between EVs and the grid: when parked, sell power back; when charging, draw from the grid. The car is transport and distributed storage.

Beyond power-market liberalization we need reserve capacity and less friction than ‘only Taipower can buy,’ so household rooftops and EVs connect faster.

Rough math: a ~75 kWh car, if government scaled fleets, at 10 kW peak for six hours—300,000 vehicles ≈ 3 GW. Full load at Taichung coal is about 5.5 GW—enough to discuss ‘running half as many coal units.’

Funding could come from ‘anti-pollution EV bonds’: vehicles earn peak/off-peak spreads while being offered cheap or free to families with young children—thanks for raising the next taxpayers.

EV energy cost is about a tenth of ICE; engine pollution is a different order of magnitude. EVs can also absorb unstable offshore wind. Air policy that never talks storage and finance structure will not land.
