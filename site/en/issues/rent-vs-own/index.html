17 Renting vs owning

Deep finance can mean less forced homebuying.

Only about 40% of Swiss live in owner-occupied homes; sixty percent rent. In the financial crisis, finance-centered Switzerland was relatively less damaged; its housing structure differs from the U.S.—less extreme premium and speculation.

East Asia often holds ‘land is wealth’: fewer financial products, retirement leaning on property. Where markets are deep and products rich, people need not stake a whole life on one building.

Swiss apartment rents around NT$30–40k/month can be relatively affordable versus income; a large share of rental supply comes from private owners, and public rental is higher than many Taiwanese imagine.

That does not mean Taiwan should ‘all switch to renting.’ Where financial asset markets are still shallow, buying remains self-protection. The point is: rentable, buyable, and more than one survival script.

Housing policy that only shouts ‘a home for everyone’ without deepening the rental market and financial alternatives often ends as slogan and disappointment.
