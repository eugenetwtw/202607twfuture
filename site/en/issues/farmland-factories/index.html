16 Farmland factories and supply chains

Use data and standards, not slogans.

Taiwan’s farmland factories are an old problem rarely quantified: how much tax? How many jobs? How much external cost in wastewater and emissions?

COVID in 2020 shattered global supply chains; production sites and backups were revalued. If Taiwan only fights ‘illegal vs legal,’ it misses real trade-offs between industry and land.

Aerial views of farmland and suspected illegal builds are landscape and a snapshot of policy failure—not one greed, but long co-responsibility among enforcement, planning, and local economies.

The pandemic proved strict control can work. The same capacity can set environmental standards: stay in the supply chain only if you meet wastewater, emissions, and safety rules—not blanket wipeout or blanket laissez-faire.

Land policy is industrial policy. Driving factories from invisible farmland to worse places does not fix pollution or food safety; putting standards and data on the table is how a modern state starts.
